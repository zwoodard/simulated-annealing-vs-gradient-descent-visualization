Vanilla GD

Follows the slope downhill. Always gets trapped in the nearest local minimum. Sensitive to gradient scale: too high overshoots, too low crawls.

GD + Momentum

Accumulates velocity like a rolling ball. Low momentum = responsive, can overshoot bumps while moving fast. High momentum = heavy, resists direction changes. Try 0.3-0.6 to escape small hills.
1.0 = no decay. Lower = shrinks LR over time so it settles.

Adam

Effective step size, auto-scaled by gradient history.
How much past gradient direction is remembered.
How much past gradient magnitude is remembered.
Combines momentum with adaptive step sizing. Divides by the running average of gradient magnitudes, so the LR behaves consistently regardless of how steep the function is. This is why Adam's LR is much smaller — it self-adjusts.

Simulated Annealing

How far each random jump can go.
Higher = more willing to accept uphill moves early on.
How quickly temperature drops. Closer to 1 = slower cooling.
Not gradient-based at all. Jumps randomly, accepts bad moves probabilistically. Explores broadly when hot, settles when cold. Inspired by metalworking annealing.
