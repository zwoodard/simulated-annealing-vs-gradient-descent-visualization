Follows the slope downhill. Always gets trapped in the nearest local minimum. Sensitive to gradient scale: too high overshoots, too low crawls.
Accumulates velocity like a rolling ball. Low momentum = responsive, can overshoot bumps while moving fast. High momentum = heavy, resists direction changes. Try 0.3–0.6 to escape small hills. LR Decay: 1.0 = none, lower = settles over time.
Combines momentum with adaptive step sizing. Divides by running average of gradient magnitudes, so the LR stays consistent regardless of steepness. This is why Adam's LR is much smaller—it self-adjusts.
Not gradient-based. Jumps randomly, accepts bad moves probabilistically. Explores broadly when hot, settles when cold. Inspired by metalworking annealing.
